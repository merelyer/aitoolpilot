Video editing in 2025 is a battlefield of two philosophies: traditional powerhouse versus AI-first innovation. On one side, DaVinci Resolve remains the gold standard for professional color grading, multi-track editing, and studio-grade post-production—all for free. DaVinci Resolve 18.6: The Traditional Powerhouse

DaVinci Resolve has evolved from a color grading tool into a full-fledged NLE (non-linear editor) used by Hollywood studios. The 2025 version (18.6) introduces AI-powered features like DaVinci Neural Engine for object tracking, scene cut detection, and voice isolation—but it’s still fundamentally a manual, precision-driven tool.

Key AI Features in DaVinci Resolve 18.6

  • Object Tracking: AI-driven point and planar tracking for masks and effects.
  • Scene Cut Detection: Automatically splits clips based on cuts (great for multi-cam).
  • Voice Isolation: Removes background noise using neural networks.
  • Smart Reframe: Auto-crops for vertical/social formats.
  • Speed Warp: AI optical flow for smooth slow-motion.

Runway ML (Gen-3): The AI-First Innovator

Runway ML is not a traditional video editor—it’s a cloud-based AI platform for generating and editing video using machine learning models. The Gen-3 model (2025) can create 10-second clips from text, remove objects, and even animate still images. It’s built for speed and creativity, not precision.

Key AI Features in Runway ML Gen-3

  • Text-to-Video: Generate video clips from text prompts (up to 10 seconds).
  • Inpainting: Remove objects or people from video with a brush.
  • Motion Brush: Animate specific areas of a still image.
  • Green Screen (AI): Auto-keying with no need for physical greenscreen.
  • Frame Interpolation: AI slow-motion and frame generation.

Who Should Use What

Choose DaVinci Resolve if:

  • You’re editing a feature film, documentary, or commercial.
  • You need precise color grading and audio mixing.
  • You prefer a one-time purchase over subscriptions.
  • You have a powerful PC/Mac and time to learn.

Choose Runway ML if:

  • You create short-form content for TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube Shorts.
  • You want to generate video from text prompts.
  • You need to quickly remove objects or backgrounds without keyframing.
  • You’re a designer, marketer, or AI enthusiast, not a professional editor.

Bottom Line Verdict

DaVinci Resolve remains the king of traditional editing—it’s free, powerful, and trusted by professionals. Runway ML is the future of generative video, but it’s not a replacement for a full NLE. The smartest workflow in 2025 is to use both: edit and grade in DaVinci, then jump to Runway for AI effects. If you can only pick one, ask yourself: do you need control or creativity? Control = DaVinci. Creativity = Runway.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can DaVinci Resolve generate video from text?

No. DaVinci Resolve does not have a text-to-video feature. It relies on manual editing and traditional AI tools like object tracking and scene detection. For generative AI video, you need Runway ML or similar tools.

Is Runway ML better than DaVinci Resolve for color grading?

No. Runway ML has basic color tools but is not designed for professional color grading. DaVinci Resolve is the industry leader with unlimited nodes, HDR grading, and advanced scopes.

Which is cheaper: DaVinci Resolve or Runway ML?

DaVinci Resolve is cheaper long-term. The free version is fully functional, and Studio is $295 one-time. Runway ML requires a subscription ($15-$95/month) and credits can run out quickly.

Can I use DaVinci Resolve and Runway ML together?

Yes. Many professionals export clips from DaVinci, process them in Runway for AI effects (like object removal or generative fill), then re-import them into DaVinci for final assembly and grading.

Does Runway ML have a free version?

Yes. The free tier gives 125 credits per month (one text-to-video clip can cost 5-10 credits), 1080p export, and watermarks on exports. It’s enough to test the platform.

Which tool is better for YouTube videos?

DaVinci Resolve is better for long-form YouTube videos with multiple edits, color grading, and audio. Runway ML is great for short-form or AI-generated clips. Many YouTubers use both.

Ready to choose?

If you’re leaning toward DaVinci Resolve, download the free version here (or buy Studio for $295). If Runway ML sounds like your speed, try Runway ML free and upgrade to Pro when you need more credits. Both tools offer something unique—your choice depends on your workflow.
