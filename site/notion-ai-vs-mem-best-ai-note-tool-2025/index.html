Detailed Review: Notion AI

Notion AI isn't a separate tool—it's an artificial intelligence layer on top of the already popular Notion workspace. Launched in early 2023, it has matured significantly by 2025. Instead of just organizing your notes, it can write, summarize, edit, brainstorm, and even generate action items from your content.

Key Features

  • AI Writing Assistant: Generate drafts, rewrite paragraphs, change tone (professional, casual, concise), translate, and fix spelling/grammar.
  • Smart Summarization: Summarize long notes, meeting notes, or entire databases with one click.
  • AI Autofill: Automatically populate database properties (e.g., generate a summary, extract key dates, or categorize entries).
  • Q&A Across Workspace: Ask questions like “What were the action items from last week’s meeting?” and get answers pulled from your pages.
  • Brainstorming & Ideation: Generate blog post outlines, project ideas, or creative prompts.
  • Translation: Translate notes into multiple languages.

Pricing

Notion has a generous free tier for personal use. The AI add-on costs $10 per member per month (billed annually) or $10/month when added to a paid Notion plan. Alternatively, you can get the Notion + AI bundle for $18 per member per month (billed annually), which includes the full workspace features plus AI. There’s also a free trial with 20 AI responses.

Pros

  • Deep integration with Notion’s powerful databases, wikis, and project management.
  • Works across your entire workspace—not just single pages.
  • Excellent for structured, collaborative environments (teams, agencies, startups).
  • Massive template library and community support.

Cons

  • AI features require an extra fee on top of Notion’s base cost.
  • Occasionally slow to generate responses, especially in large workspaces.
  • Can be overwhelming for simple note-taking—lots of features you may not need.
  • No dedicated mobile-first AI experience; the mobile app is functional but not as smooth as Mem’s.

Detailed Review: Mem

Mem is an AI-first note-taking app designed from scratch around the concept of a “second brain.” Instead of requiring you to manually organize folders, Mem uses AI to automatically link related notes, surface relevant information, and help you find anything instantly. It’s built for speed and frictionless capture.

Key Features

  • Automatic Note Linking: As you write, Mem automatically suggests connections to existing notes, creating a web of knowledge.
  • Smart Collections: AI groups related notes into collections without manual tagging.
  • Powerful Search: Search across all your notes with natural language (e.g., “show me notes from last Tuesday about marketing” ).
  • AI Writing Assistant: Similar to Notion AI—generate, rewrite, summarize, and brainstorm.
  • Quick Capture: Use the mobile app, browser extension, or desktop widget to capture ideas instantly.
  • Knowledge Graph: Visualize connections between notes, helping you discover hidden relationships.

Pricing

Mem offers a limited free tier (1,000 notes, basic AI features). The paid plan is Mem X at $14.99 per month (billed annually) or $19.99 per month monthly. This unlocks unlimited notes, full AI features, smart collections, and priority support. There’s no separate AI add-on—everything is included.

Pros

  • True AI-first experience—automatic linking and search feel magical.
  • Very fast capture; ideal for journaling, research, and quick ideas.
  • Clean, distraction-free interface.
  • No folder management required—AI organizes for you.

Cons

  • Not a full project management tool; lacks databases, calendars, and task boards.
  • Limited customization compared to Notion.
  • Smaller community and fewer third-party integrations.
  • AI linking can sometimes be inaccurate or miss context.

Who Should Use What

Choose Notion AI if:

  • You need a central workspace for notes, tasks, databases, and project management.
  • You work in a team and need real-time collaboration, comments, and permissions.
  • You love templates and want to build custom dashboards (e.g., for content planning, CRM, or OKRs).
  • You frequently write long-form content and want AI to help with drafting and editing.

Choose Mem if:

  • Your main goal is capturing ideas quickly and retrieving them effortlessly.
  • You hate organizing and want AI to automatically link and surface relevant notes.
  • You are a researcher, writer, or knowledge worker who deals with high volumes of notes.
  • You prefer a minimalist, distraction-free interface over a feature-packed workspace.

Bottom Line Verdict

Both Notion AI and Mem are excellent tools, but they serve different primary use cases. Notion AI wins for versatility, collaboration, and structured work. It’s the Swiss Army knife of productivity. Mem wins for pure note-taking speed and AI-driven organization—it’s like having a personal librarian. If you can afford both, consider using Notion for projects and Mem for personal notes. But if you must choose one, go with Notion AI if you need an all-in-one solution, or Mem if your life revolves around notes and you want AI to do the heavy lifting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Notion AI free?

Notion has a free plan for the workspace itself, but the AI add-on costs extra ($10 per member per month). There is a free trial with 20 AI responses.

Does Mem have a mobile app?

Yes, Mem offers native mobile apps for iOS and Android, optimized for quick capture and search.

Which tool is better for team collaboration?

Notion AI is far superior for teams, offering shared databases, permissions, comments, and real-time editing. Mem is more individual-focused, though it does support basic sharing.

Can I export my notes from Mem or Notion?

Yes, both allow export. Notion supports Markdown, HTML, CSV, and PDF. Mem supports Markdown and JSON export.

Which tool has better AI writing features?

Both have similar writing capabilities (generate, rewrite, summarize). Notion AI’s advantage is that it can work across databases and pages, while Mem focuses on individual notes and collections.

Is Mem secure for business use?

Mem uses encryption in transit and at rest, but it lacks enterprise-grade features like SSO, audit logs, or advanced permissions. Notion offers more robust security for businesses.
