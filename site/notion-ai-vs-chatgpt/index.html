Detailed Review: Notion AI

What is Notion AI?

Notion AI is an add-on layer (or a built-in feature if you subscribe to a paid Notion plan) that brings generative AI directly into your pages, databases, and wikis. Instead of jumping to a separate app, you can ask Notion to draft a blog post, summarize a long note, generate action items from a meeting transcript, or even translate text—all within the same interface where you manage your projects.

Key Features

  • Inline AI writing: Press space or highlight text to get suggestions, rewrites, summaries, or continuations.
  • Q&A over your workspace: Ask questions like “What were the action items from last week’s sprint?” and Notion AI scans your pages to answer.
  • Database automations: Generate property values, formulas, or even entire database entries using AI prompts.
  • Project management integration: Automatically create tasks, assign owners, and set due dates from natural language instructions.
  • Customizable AI templates: Pre-built prompts for meeting notes, project plans, and more.

Pricing

Notion AI is an add-on to your Notion plan. You can get it as a $10/month per member bolt-on to any paid Notion plan (Plus, Business, or Enterprise). Alternatively, if you subscribe to Notion Plus ($12/month per member) and add AI, you're paying $22/month per member total. There's a free trial of Notion AI for all users, but it's limited.

Pros

  • Deeply integrated: AI works exactly where you already write and organize—no context switching.
  • Workspace-aware: It can reference your existing notes, databases, and pages, making answers more relevant.
  • Great for teams: Shared AI features help standardize documentation and reduce repetitive work.

Cons

  • Expensive for individual users: If you don't already use Notion, the cost adds up (Notion base plan + AI add-on).
  • AI quality varies: Sometimes the AI hallucinates or gives generic responses, especially for complex queries.
  • Limited creative freedom: Not as good as ChatGPT for long-form, open-ended creative writing or role-playing.

Detailed Review: ChatGPT

What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is OpenAI's conversational AI model (now GPT-4o as of 2025) that you can access via web, mobile app, or API. It's a general-purpose AI assistant that excels at generating text, answering questions, writing code, brainstorming, and even analyzing uploaded files (images, PDFs, spreadsheets). It's not tied to any specific productivity suite, so you use it as a standalone tool.

Key Features

  • Conversational interface: Chat naturally, ask follow-ups, and refine outputs iteratively.
  • Multimodal capabilities: Upload images, PDFs, or data files and ask ChatGPT to analyze or extract information.
  • Custom GPTs: Create specialized versions of ChatGPT for specific tasks (e.g., “Marketing Copywriter,” “Code Reviewer”).
  • Web browsing and plugins: Access real-time information and integrate with third-party services (though plugins are being phased out in favor of GPTs).
  • Voice conversations: Speak to ChatGPT and get spoken responses (mobile app).

Pricing

ChatGPT has a generous free tier (GPT-4o mini, with limited messages). ChatGPT Plus costs $20/month and gives you priority access to GPT-4o, longer context, and more features. ChatGPT Pro (for heavy users) is $200/month and includes unlimited access to the most advanced models. Teams and Enterprise plans are also available.

Pros

  • Exceptional creative and coding ability: Outperforms Notion AI in generating long-form content, debugging code, and brainstorming.
  • Flexible and standalone: Use it anywhere—browser, mobile, API—without being tied to a specific ecosystem.
  • Regularly updated: OpenAI pushes new models and features frequently.

Cons

  • No native workspace integration: You have to copy-paste or manually transfer outputs to your notes or project management tool.
  • No memory of your specific projects: It doesn't know your Notion databases or team context unless you manually provide them.
  • Can be distracting: It's a separate app, so it's easy to fall into a rabbit hole of unrelated queries.

Who Should Use What

Choose Notion AI if...

  • You already use Notion for notes, project management, or knowledge base.
  • You work in a team that needs AI to summarize meetings, generate tasks, or maintain consistent documentation.
  • You want AI that understands your existing data (e.g., “Summarize the Q1 marketing plan from the database”).
  • You value a single, integrated workspace over having multiple tabs open.

Choose ChatGPT if...

  • You need a powerful, standalone AI for creative writing, coding, or brainstorming.
  • You don't use Notion (or use a different productivity tool) and want a flexible AI assistant.
  • You frequently work with uploaded files (images, PDFs, spreadsheets) for analysis.
  • You want the latest and greatest AI model without paying per user (just a flat subscription).

Bottom Line Verdict

Both Notion AI and ChatGPT are excellent tools, but they serve different primary use cases. Notion AI is the better choice if you're looking to embed intelligence into your existing workflow—it reduces context switching and makes your notes and databases smarter. ChatGPT is the better choice if you need a powerful, standalone AI that can handle diverse creative and analytical tasks without being tied to a specific platform.

For most professionals and teams, I recommend starting with Notion AI because it elevates your entire productivity environment. If you find you need more creative firepower or file analysis, you can always supplement with ChatGPT's free tier or a $20/month Plus subscription.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is Notion AI better than ChatGPT for writing?

Not necessarily. ChatGPT is generally more creative and coherent for long-form writing like blog posts, stories, or emails. Notion AI is better for writing that's integrated into your workspace—like meeting notes, project descriptions, or database entries. For pure writing quality, ChatGPT wins; for contextual writing inside your workflow, Notion AI wins.

2. Can Notion AI replace ChatGPT?

No, not entirely. Notion AI is designed to augment your existing Notion workspace, not to be a standalone conversational assistant. ChatGPT offers broader capabilities (multimodal inputs, custom GPTs, web browsing) that Notion AI doesn't have. However, for many daily productivity tasks, Notion AI can reduce your reliance on ChatGPT.

3. Which is more affordable: Notion AI or ChatGPT?

For an individual, ChatGPT Plus at $20/month is cheaper than Notion AI ($10/month add-on) if you already have a free Notion account. But if you need a paid Notion plan (e.g., Plus at $12/month) plus AI, the total is $22/month per person. For teams, Notion AI can be cost-effective because it replaces multiple tools. ChatGPT Pro at $200/month is only for power users.

4. Does Notion AI have a free trial?

Yes, Notion offers a limited free trial of Notion AI for all users. You get a set number of AI responses (usually around 50) to test the feature. After that, you need a paid subscription. ChatGPT's free tier is more generous, but it uses a less powerful model (GPT-4o mini).

5. Can I use ChatGPT with Notion?

Yes, you can use ChatGPT alongside Notion. Many users copy-paste ChatGPT responses into Notion pages. There are also third-party integrations (like Zapier) that connect ChatGPT to Notion, but they're not native. Notion AI offers a more seamless experience because it's built directly into the editor.

6. Which tool is better for project management?

Notion AI is far better for project management because it's integrated with Notion's databases, timelines, and task lists. You can ask it to create tasks, update statuses, or summarize project progress. ChatGPT can help you plan a project, but it can't manage one—you'd need to manually input everything into your project management tool.

7. Are there any privacy concerns with Notion AI vs ChatGPT?

Both tools use your data to improve their models unless you opt out (ChatGPT) or are on a business plan (Notion). Notion AI processes your content on Notion's servers, and OpenAI processes your conversations on theirs. For sensitive data, check each company's data usage policies. Notion's Enterprise plan offers data processing agreements, and OpenAI's API doesn't use data for training by default.
