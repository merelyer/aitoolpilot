Detailed Reviews of Each Tool

1. Midjourney

Overview: Midjourney has evolved from a niche Discord bot to a full-fledged web app. Its latest version (v6.5) delivers stunning photorealism and artistic styles that rival stock photography.

Key Features: Inpainting/outpainting, style references, image-to-image, and a new 'Describe' feature that writes prompts for you. Commercial rights are granted on all paid plans.

Pricing: Basic Plan: $10/month (3.3 hours of GPU time). Standard: $30/month (15 hours). Pro: $60/month (30 hours). Enterprise: custom.

Pros: Unmatched artistic quality, active community, constant updates. Cons: Steep learning curve, no built-in editing tools, requires Discord (though web app is rolling out).

2. Adobe Firefly

Overview: Adobe's generative AI is built into Photoshop, Illustrator, and Express. It's designed for professionals who need legally safe, commercially licensed images with full indemnification.

Key Features: Generative Fill, text effects, vector recoloring, and integration with Adobe Stock. The Firefly Enterprise plan offers IP indemnification.

Pricing: Firefly Free: 25 credits/month. Firefly Premium: $4.99/month (100 credits). Enterprise: custom pricing (usually $20+/user/month).

Pros: Legally safe, seamless with Adobe tools, easy to use. Cons: Limited style variety compared to Midjourney, credit system can be restrictive for heavy users.

3. Leonardo AI

Overview: Leonardo AI is a rising star known for its generous free tier and strong community. It offers fine-tuned models for specific styles (e.g., RPG, anime, photorealistic).

Key Features: Real-time generation, image-to-image, canvas editor, and model training. Commercial use is allowed on all plans, including free.

Pricing: Free: 150 tokens/day. Starter: $10/month (1,500 tokens). Pro: $25/month (4,500 tokens). Unlimited: $60/month.

Pros: Very affordable, great for game assets and concept art. Cons: Interface can be cluttered, some models produce inconsistent results.

4. DALL·E 3 (via ChatGPT Plus)

Overview: OpenAI's latest image generator is integrated into ChatGPT Plus. It excels at understanding complex prompts and generating accurate, detailed images.

Key Features: Text-to-image, editing via conversation, and seamless integration with ChatGPT. Commercial rights are granted for images created with a paid account.

Pricing: ChatGPT Plus: $20/month (includes DALL·E 3 access with a daily limit). No standalone DALL·E plan.

Pros: Easiest to use, great for quick mockups. Cons: Limited control over style, lower resolution than competitors, daily cap.

5. Stable Diffusion (via DreamStudio)

Overview: The open-source powerhouse. DreamStudio offers a user-friendly web interface, while advanced users can run models locally for full control.

Key Features: Custom checkpoints, ControlNet, inpainting, and unlimited generation if self-hosted. Commercial use depends on the model license (most are permissive).

Pricing: DreamStudio: $10/month (1,000 credits). Self-hosted: free (requires GPU).

Pros: Maximum customizability, no censorship, community models. Cons: Requires technical knowledge for local setup, quality varies by model.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sell images generated by AI?

Yes, with most paid plans from Midjourney, Adobe Firefly, Leonardo AI, and DALL·E 3, you own the commercial rights to the images you generate. Always check the specific tool's terms of service.

Which AI image generator has the best commercial license?

Adobe Firefly offers the strongest commercial license with full indemnification, meaning Adobe will defend you if someone claims copyright infringement. Midjourney and Leonardo AI also provide clear commercial rights but without indemnification.

Is DALL·E 3 free for commercial use?

DALL·E 3 is not free for commercial use. You need a paid ChatGPT Plus subscription ($20/month) to generate images with commercial rights. The free tier of ChatGPT only allows non-commercial use.

What is the best free AI image generator for commercial use?

Leonardo AI's free tier is the best option. It provides 150 daily tokens and grants commercial rights to all images created. Stable Diffusion (self-hosted) is also free but requires technical setup.

Can I use AI-generated images for my business logo?

Yes, but be cautious. Most tools allow commercial use, but AI-generated logos may not be unique enough for trademark protection. It's best to use AI as a starting point and then refine with a designer.

Which AI image generator is best for photorealistic product shots?

Midjourney v6.5 excels at photorealism. For product-specific shots, Adobe Firefly's generative fill in Photoshop allows you to integrate AI elements into real product photos seamlessly.
